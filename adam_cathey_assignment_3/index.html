Adam Cathey

(and the people he’s been)

Paths Sheres Communications

One of my best friends, my psuedo brother, Dan, has this thing he says about me: "of all the people I know, you’ve been the most".
This website is an attempt to map out what he might be on about (in chronological order).

the personæ∴

א.The Joker/Jester ↑

1

When I was very young the first person I remember being was a mischievous yet charming trickster. Always doing impressions and bringing my imagination to the extremes of absurdity for the sake of a laugh. My family enjoyed it and I still make my mom laugh 'till she's crying.

ב.The Knight/Ninja ↑

2

Another early way persona was this hyper moral kid who was willing to fight for honor and justice. This was also very young, and I was mostly just involved in typical boyhood imaginary conflicts with the mythical ghosts and bad guys of the neighbourhood (I think there was a killer clown in the forest near my house).

ג.The Troubadour ↑

3

This knightly character also took on the character of a romantic duty to protect whatever princess of my third-grade class I happened to have a crush on. I took to writing poetry and doing grand gestures and fashioning sacred artifacts and amulets to win the heart of the queen. Standard Super Mario gallantry.

ד.The Sporty Kid ↑

4

Very brief period where I was really into soccer and basketball. Channeling my mercurial character into sport gave me the superpower of always popping up out of nowhere, weaving in and out of players and always doing the unexpected. I relied on dynamism and willingness to debase myself, as I lacked the type of discipline that would make me a serious player. I thought martial arts was more of my sport anyway...

ה.The Cool Kid ↑

5

Doing sports at that age was synonymous with being part of the less-academically inclined "cool kid class". We listened to gangsta rap, talked about sex and smoked,probably even drank a few OE’s. Fighting a less than noble fight was not my scene, and when a new kid on the block came along vying for my place in the social hierarchy, I declined his "petty contest" and found myself a loser by default,I supposed my social group didn't have the same bedrock of Christian chivalric morality that I did, so it wasn't the powerplay that I'd imagined it would be.

ו.The Arty Kid ↑

6

Next, I found community in a small circle of creative kids. They had similar dropout attitudes, but were geeky non-conformist, all very unique. I started drawing comics a lot and getting more into things like poetry and fine art (appreciating it). I even started listening to folk, jazz and classical music... eschewing the hip-hop as the 'language of the oppressors'.

ז.The Whiny Emo ↑

7

At the time there was no 'emo' proper, but we were that avant la lettre…Wearing make-up, obsession with the occult and horror movies, loud punk and metal music, depression and angst. I wore it on my sleeve, and I identified with all the wussy/whiney things that the bros hated.

ח.The Hermit ↑

8

Depression became a spiritual calling, I chose renunciation of the 'idiot world', dawned my garbs of hip styled bohemian esoterica, and read 'Dharma Bums' with mala beads around my wrists... I actually went to poetry readings and presented as the isolated mystic who has crawled out from his hovel to share his melancholic revelations. Maybe that crossed with Nietzche Zarathustra character, pretentious as fuck.

ט.Andy Kaufman ↑

9

There was a time where I was actually very interested in consciously playing with identity, as a type of living theatre. This phase I actually did radical experiments with myself, changing big things in the ways that I dressed, my manner of behaving and also my perception of the world. I had been opening the doors of perception for a long time in line with standard hippy pharmacology, but now I was moving towards existential psychodrama. Usually left my friends a little confused.

י.The Satanist ↑

10

I was really into the whole 'satanic scare' thing of the eighties, and I actually agreed with most of their philosophies, so I identified with this for a while. Probably just to piss off my parents and everything/one they stood for. I was never into silly moribund graveyard stuff or anything, just toted Crowley books around with me and wanted my friends to summon shit on our playdates.

כ.The Radical ↑

11

Around 9/11 there was a furry of activist organization going on in my city and I went to every rally, teach-in and anarchist or communist study group I could find. This didn't bode to well with my fairly reactionary and disengaged taste for metaphysics, but there was a revolution happening any minute, and I definitely wanted to be on the vanguard.

ל.The Drug Addict ↑

12

All this intensity took its toll on my mental health and relationships. I found solace in drugs, and yeah, I went off the deepend for a bit.

מ.The Buddhist Monk ↑

13

Tried to get off of drugs by fierce devotion to my spiritual path as I had been disillusioned by the activist life. But it was too easy to keep my addiction going while living with my Lama. I lived and studied under him for two years but couldn't kick drugs (which I would leave at night to do in secret to do). When it came time to actually enter the monastery, I ran. I needed some more time to explore the underworld, I guess.

נ.The Peace Monk ↑

14

I then joined with a Christian fundamentalist recovery community. Though, I didn't like most of what they believed and did I was afforded a quiet space to practice and study in relative isolation in a giant and nearly empty monastery in the Canadian Maritimes. I studied the Christian mystical tradition as well as its pacifist anti-establishment tradition, much of which I identified with for years, while keeping to my essentially Buddhist practice. Things like the Catholic Workers Movement, Thomas Merton and proto-anarchist stuff like Leo Tolstoy and the Anabaptist tradition were my jam for a while.

ס.The Jew ↑

15

I discovered Jewish heritage and wore a Yakama for a bit. Tried to learn Hebrew, tried to go to Israel to connect with that tradition. I'm still Jewish, but not really culturally at all.

ע.The Mod ↑

16

Left the monastery, but kept this heavy disciplined ascetic lifestyle, coupled with the shaved head and an affinity for working class identity I began to identify with the traditional skinhead style (non-racist). I even got a scooter and dressed like an old timey factory worker, or maybe a black bumper Mennonite.

פ.The Queer Punk ↑

17

Got really into the Queer Punk scene for a bit. Played in a few bands and made the scene. Flags hanging out of my pocket and a Chelsea hairdo.Dirty white shoes, tank tops.

צ.The Burglar ↑

18

Really liked dressing like a burglar and talking about how the lumpenproletariat were the key revolutionary class that Marx and Engels overlooked. A return to reading Sartre and Genet from my radical phase. This phase lasted a long time.

ק.The Stagehand ↑

19

I worked as a stage hand and in film production for a long time, and LOOKED THE PART, this profession has a definite ethos and aesthetic.

ר.The Boss ↑

20

I started my own small A/V company after a few years of being crew lead for the productions I was working on. I had a warehouse and a few trucks. I was a workaholic and a pretty stern guy. Tons of coffee, smoking, clipboards and always on my phone.

ש.The Hippy ↑

21

Burnout from overwork, I started to fold my company. Took less gigs, eventually ended up deciding to quit working and live in my truck, making art. I lived near parks in the city and spent a lot of time enjoying nature and folk music, as well as rediscovering my passion for philosophy and literature.

ת.The Techy ↑

22

My years of working with audio-visual technology and my long-time interest in new science brought me more and more back to studying new computing technology. Working in digital art also, I decided to forsake the more material-based work that I had done all my life and pursue a career in technology, specifically programming. My philosophy even took a digital turn, and I was more interested in things like cyber utopias,stacktivismand having computational intervention into sociological problems and even the traditional all-to-human work of philosophy and science.

֍.Student ↑

23

So After 21 years of being out of school, I went back to study programming. This is the phase I'm in now. My style is basic, I keep things on an even keel, and I'm just interested in being as healthy as I can be-not stressing about work or the revolution, or wildly experimental lifestyles. Minimalism and clarity of purpose is my aim. And humbly working toward a place where I can have more leisure to build transformative systems and environments both through art and technology.So far my biggest challenge is Base2 Base16 mathematics.
